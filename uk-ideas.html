Our Ideas for the UK

These are not dreams. These are legal, actionable reforms — possible under current UK law or with parliamentary change. We will introduce them when we govern.

Our Promise: No power without accountability. No reform without consent. No future without youth.

🗳️ 1. Youth Voting at 13 (With Verification)

Lower voting age to 13 — but only after understanding verification.

  • A neutral Civic Guide asks: “Who are you voting for and why?”
  • If the answer shows real understanding → vote allowed
  • If answer is “everyone else is” or “I don’t know” → guided education, no vote
  • Legal basis: Voting age is not in law — set by Parliament. Precedent: Scotland allows 16+ voting

🤝 2. Co-Governance Networks (Local Power)

Let youth form co-govs to solve local issues — with real authority.

  • Any 16+ group can form a co-gov (school, town, online)
  • Decisions binding if supported by majority of affected community
  • Funded by local councils via youth civic budget
  • Legal basis: Localism Act 2011 allows community decision-making

🛡️ 3. Child Safety Support (Not Control)

Protect high-risk children — humanely and legally.

  • Free home safety kits for families with children (0–10) with severe autism or self-harm risk:
    • Padded bed surrounds
    • Floor crash mats
    • Door alarms
  • Provided via NHS/SEND services — voluntary
  • If risk is clear and help is refused → safeguarding review opened
  • Legal basis: Children Act 1989 — duty to protect from significant harm

🔍 4. Truth Access & Social Media Reform

Social media must serve truth — not engagement.

  • Require platforms to offer a “Truth Mode” algorithm:
    • Prioritizes fact-checked sources
    • Shows multiple perspectives
    • Labels AI content clearly
  • Mandate “Why am I seeing this?” transparency tags
  • Legal basis: Online Safety Act 2023 — can be expanded to include algorithmic fairness

🏡 5. Rooted Families Support

Help families with deep UK roots and low income — with verification.

  • Households with 4+ generations born in the UK and income under £30k/year can apply
  • Support includes:
    • Home resilience grants (boilers, insulation)
    • Kids’ learning boost (tutors, devices)
    • Food & essentials boxes
  • Verified by in-person Community Support Officer (not DWP)
  • Legal basis: Local authorities can target support under Community Strategy duties

🛃 6. Fair & Fast Asylum System

No free rides. No endless delays. No harm to the public.

  • Arrival by small boat → automatic processing within 28 days
  • No access to benefits, housing, or non-urgent NHS care during review
  • If claim denied → deportation within 60 days
  • If granted asylum → no welfare access for 10 years (must work and pay taxes first)
  • Legal basis: Nationality and Borders Act 2022 allows differentiated access

👨‍👩‍👧 7. Adoption-Only Stay for Children in Care

No automatic citizenship. No long-term state care.

  • If a child arrives with parents who are deported, they can only stay if:
    • A UK family fully adopts them, or
    • They are safely reunited with family in a safe home country
  • No indefinite foster care for irregular entrants
  • Legal basis: Best interests of the child (ECHR, UNCRC) — but no right to stay

⚖️ 8. Self-Correcting Government

Power must be earned — and can be lost.

  • Leaders sign a public covenant at 18 — breaking core rules triggers automatic recall vote
  • Guardian Council (7 independent citizens) monitors integrity
  • If 3 red flags or 50% of co-govs vote no confidence → binding leadership review
  • Legal basis: Recall of MPs Act 2015 — can be expanded to party leaders
We will not promise what we cannot do.
But we will fight for every legal, fair, and humane reform — until the UK works for all.
